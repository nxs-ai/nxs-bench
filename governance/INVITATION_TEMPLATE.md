# Advisory Board Invitation Template

## Outreach Goal

Invite 8-10 candidates and target 3-5 acceptances from:

- top contributors to the repository
- authors of related benchmarks such as tau-bench and FACTS Grounding
- AI agent researchers
- enterprise AI practitioners

Timeline target: form the board within 6 months of the NXS-Bench v1.0 launch,
contingent on reaching the 10-contribution threshold.

## Invitation Email

**Subject:** Invitation to join the NXS-Bench Advisory Board

Hello {{name}},

I am reaching out on behalf of NXS-Bench, the open benchmark suite for AI
agent evaluation. We are forming an external Advisory Board to ensure the
benchmark remains fair, unbiased, and scientifically rigorous as community
adoption grows.

We are inviting a small group of external members from research, competing or
adjacent agent platforms, and enterprise practice. The board's role is to
review new benchmark domains, methodology changes, scoring rubric updates, and
scenario retirement or rotation policies. The board also has veto power over
changes that would advantage NXS unfairly.

The commitment is intentionally light: quarterly meetings plus async voting via
GitHub Issues, for roughly **2-4 hours/quarter**.

Why this board exists:

- NXS-Bench should not ask the community to trust a benchmark that NXS controls
  without independent oversight.
- Public governance, public minutes, and external veto power make the benchmark
  more credible for everyone who uses it.

What you would receive:

- a public governance role on the benchmark
- early review of major methodology changes
- visibility into benchmark roadmap discussions before public release

If you are interested, I would be happy to share the charter and answer any
questions. If not, I would still appreciate referrals to others who would be a
strong fit.

Best,

{{sender_name}}
{{sender_title}}
{{sender_contact}}

## Recruitment Process

1. Build a candidate list across the target groups above.
2. Prioritize candidates with public credibility and willingness to be named.
3. Send the invitation with the charter attached or linked.
4. Track responses privately until members accept.
5. Publish accepted members in `governance/MEMBERS.md`.
